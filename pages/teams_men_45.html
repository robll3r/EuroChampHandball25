SAGRADA FAMILIA - SPAIN

The Sagrada Familia Club has over 40 years of history and was the first sports club registered in the Register of Sports Entities of the Andalusian Regional Government in the province of Malaga.
Throughout its history, it has had a large youth handball academy, as well as teams in both men's and women's national categories.
Currently, the club is focused on a veteran handball project to offer players who no longer compete officially the opportunity to continue enjoying the sport and competition at a higher level.

ESFERA ANDEBOL - PORTUGAL

Founded in 2012, our club began with a men's veterans handball team, born from a deep passion for the sport and a strong sense of camaraderie and competition. Our beginning as a team officially dates back to the 4th of February 2010, when several players and managers of different teams belonging to the Catalan Handball Federation got together to participate in the European Championship organized on the 18th, 19th and 20th of June in the town of Berga (Catalonia).

We are extremely excited to be able to visit your "magical" city next June, enjoy your well-known hospitality and enjoy, one more year, our beloved sport with the rest of the participating teams.

GRANADA - SPAIN

The Granada Veterans Handball Club began its journey in 2016 as an initiative of a group of fans of this sport with the idea of remembering ancient times and promoting the practice of Handball in its Veteran modality.
Granada is a province with a long handball tradition, which caused former colleagues from various clubs in the province of Granada to quickly join.
With the passion for this sport as our standard and with a third time always up to par, we try to leave our stamp in each of the tournaments we participate in.

OSUNA - SPAIN

The members of the current Osuna Madrid Veterans team have largely grown and aged with Club Osuna Madrid, since its founding in 1993, and even before. The seed of this Osuna Madrid team was Club BACOAL, founded by a group of players in 1991, some of whom are still fighting on the courts of Madrid, wearing (more tightly) the Osuna Madrid Veterans Team jersey.
We started in the early nineties, in the Madrid youth categories, rising from the 2nd Territorial Division to the 2nd National Division, and even reaching the 1st National Division in 2002. This experience was only enjoyed for one year, and with the help of the municipality of Arganda (since the club's structure at that time couldn't afford the outlay that this challenge entailed).
Unfortunately, the experience was brief and ended with relegation to the 2nd National Division and the following year (a player exodus) to the 1st Territorial Division. But we were able to recover and return to the 2nd National Division, and since then we have always maintained that category, including participation in national promotion sectors, until the arrival of Covid. Throughout this period, we have obviously seen the addition and departure of players, but we can say, with a certain pride, that we have been able to maintain a fairly stable core, in which the friendship has grown even beyond our love for handball.
For this reason, and after overcoming the mandatory period of confinement, we decided to take a step back, and leave the 2nd National team in the hands of the Club's young players, and we created the new Veterans team, with which we continue to enjoy Handball (at a speed appropriate for our physique), and it allows us to continue having the excuse to get together, to play... and whatever comes up.

SANTFOST - SPAIN

The C.H. Sant Fost Veterans handball team was founded in 2002 when a group of former players got together to play in the league that had been created a year prior. Since then, we haven't stopped participating in the league, where we've been semi-finalists several times and two-time cup runners-up.
Besides the league, we've also traveled to play in tournaments like "Locos de Balonmano," which took place in Puerto Sagunto, Seville, Portimao, and various tournaments across Catalonia. In 2018, our team played its first European Championship in Innsbruck, finishing in ninth place. Since then, we've participated in the European Championship in Granollers and the European Championship in Paredes, Portugal. Next June, we'll be in Seville to play our fourth European Championship.
We hope to continue playing leagues, tournaments, European Championships, and soon celebrate the 25th anniversary of the C.H. Sant Fost Veterans team.
