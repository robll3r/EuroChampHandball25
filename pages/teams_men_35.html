TARRASA - SPAIN

It began its journey in 1995 with meetings and friendly matches organized by former club players. In 2005, the club's veterans legally established themselves as an independent entity and moved to the neighboring town of Matadepera, where the Matadepera Veterans Handball Club was founded, comprised solely of one handball team, the masters team.
Handbol Veterans Matadepera quickly established itself as one of the pioneering clubs in the Catalan Masters League of the Catalan Handball Federation (FCH). Throughout its history, the club was crowned champion of the Catalan Masters League twice: in the 2013-14 and 2015-16 seasons, and also finished runner-up in the 2014-15 and 2016-17 seasons. He also won the Catalan Masters Cup in the 2007-08 season.
At the international level, Handbol Veterans Matadepera participated in more than 10 editions of the European Masters Handball Championship organized by the EHF (European Masters Handball Championship).
The club also participated in several national and international friendly tournaments, one of the most important being the Ciudad de Sevilla International Tournament, where it always competed in the +35 category.
Throughout its recent history, Handbol Veterans Matadepera was primarily comprised of former Handbol Terrassa players, which fostered a relationship of friendship and collaboration between the two entities.
This relationship led, in 2021, to a unanimous agreement for all Handbol Veterans Matadepera members to join Club Handbol Terrassa.
Thus, starting in the 2021-2022 season, the veteran team began its journey as Handbol Terrassa Veterans and began competing in the Catalan Masters League of the Catalan Handball Federation and other competitions, marking the beginning of a new era for veteran handball in the city.

ADEMUR - SPAIN

The ADEMUR Handball Club began its journey under thame in 2007, marking its 15th anniversary. Prior to this name, the Club Balonmano Maristas de La Fuensanta was founded in 1996, which would be more than 25 years old.
After these years of the COVID pandemic, where we have suffered the loss of many players, the club is trying to rebuild itself, especially in the youth categories, by embarking on this new and exciting project. We are located at the Colegio Maristas de La Fuensanta and have been called Club Balonmano Maristas ADEMUR since 2014. The goal of establishing ourselves as a club in Murcia is to promote handball at the youth levels.
To this end, we will contact various schools in Murcia and in the surrounding areas to make handball a part of children's education, creating schools in different centers so that in the future we will have children playing handball at all levels.

CPB RENNES - FRANCE

"When we miss the top level...".
This is the idea behind our group of former handball players who played at the highest level. Driven by the same passion and taste for competition, we decided to get together for a new adventure: taking part in the European Masters Handball Championship.
Last year, when we took part for the first time, we finished in a fine 4th place. This year, the excitement is growing: there will be two teams representing France in Seville.
But beyond the results, it's an exceptional human adventure. French players from all over the world, who don't always know each other, but who share the same language: that of handball and competition.
What brings us together above all are the values we share: diversity, sharing, commitment and passing on all that the sport has to offer.
The thrill of playing at the highest level, the excitement of playing together and the passion that unites us: that's what drives us.
